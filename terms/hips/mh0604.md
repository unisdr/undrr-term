    term: Fluvial (Riverine) Flooding
    definition: >-
      Overflowing by water of the normal confines of a watercourse or other body
      of water (WMO, 2012).
    source:
      text: 'UNDRR & ISC, 2025'
      url: 'https://www.preventionweb.net/hips'
    aliases:
      - Flood
      - Flooding
    description: >-
      Whereas floods are defined in hydrology as “a rise, usually brief, in the
      water level of a stream or water body to a peak from which the water level
      recedes at a slower rate” (WMO, 2012), flooding represents the hazard
      associated with these peaks whenever the water overflows the normal
      confines of the water body. Fluvial flooding occurs over a wide range of
      river and catchment systems. Floods in river valleys occur mostly on flood
      plains or wash lands as a result of flow exceeding the capacity of the
      stream channels and spilling over the natural banks or artificial
      embankments (Fernandez, 2015).
