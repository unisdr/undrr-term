  en:
    term: Biodiversity Loss
    definition: >-
      Biodiversity loss refers to the reduction of any aspect of biological
      diversity (i.e. diversity at the genetic, species and ecosystem levels) in
      a particular area through death (including extinction), destruction or
      manual removal. It can occur at many scales, from global extinctions to
      local population extinctions, leading to a decline in total diversity at
      the same scale.
    source:
      text: 'UNDRR & ISC, 2025'

    aliases:

    description: >-
      Biodiversity loss refers to the reduction in the variety and abundance of
      life on Earth, encompassing genetic, species and ecosystem levels. This
      decline reflects a reduction in nature's functional capacity to deliver
      ecosystem services, such as pollination, climate regulation, water
      purification and nutrient cycling. It highlights the ecological
      interdependence of species and the need to maintain biodiversity for human
      wellbeing.

      Currently, more species face global extinction than ever before.
      Approximately 25% of assessed species groups are threatened, with up to 1
      million species at risk of extinction within decades unless pressures are
      reduced. Extinction rates are tens to hundreds of times higher than
      natural background rates (IPBES, 2019).
