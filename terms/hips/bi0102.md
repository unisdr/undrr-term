  en:
    term: Bloodborne Viruses
    definition: >-
      Bloodborne viruses are viruses transmitted by direct contact with infected
      blood or other body fluids (adapted from WHO, 2023).
    source:
      text: 'UNDRR & ISC, 2025'
      url: 'https://www.preventionweb.net/hips'
    aliases:
      - Blood-borne viruses
      - Blood Borne viruses
      - BBV
    description: >-
      Blood-borne viruses are viruses that some people carry in their blood, and
      which can be spread from one person to another. Those infected with a
      blood-borne virus may show little or no symptoms of serious disease, while
      other infected people may be severely ill. An infected person can transmit
      (spread) blood-borne viruses from one person to another by various routes
      and over a prolonged time period (HSE, no date a). Bloodborne pathogens
      include the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), hepatitis B virus (HBV),
      and hepatitis C virus (HCV) (CDC, 2024).
