    description: >-
      Neglected tropical diseases (NTDs) are a diverse group of
      conditions caused by a variety of pathogens (including viruses, bacteria,
      parasites, fungi and toxins) and associated with devastating health,
      social and economic consequences. NTDs are mainly prevalent among
      impoverished communities in tropical areas, although some have a much
      larger geographical distribution. It is estimated that NTDs affect more
      than 1 billion people, while the number of people requiring NTD
      interventions (both preventive and curative) is 1.6 billion (WHO, no date
      a).

      The epidemiology of NTDs is complex and often related to environmental
      conditions. Many of them are vector-borne, have animal reservoirs and are
      associated with complex life cycles. All these factors make their
      public-health control challenging (WHO, no date a).

      In addition to significant mortality and morbidity - approximately 200,000
      deaths and 19 million disability-adjusted life years (DALYs) lost
      annually, NTDs cost developing communities the equivalent of billions of
      United States dollars each year in direct health costs, loss of
      productivity and reduced socioeconomic and educational attainment. They
      are also responsible for other consequences such as disability,
      stigmatization, social exclusion and discrimination and place considerable
      financial strain on patients and their families.

      In spite of this, NTDs have historically ranked very low and almost absent
      from the global health policy agenda - only to gain recognition in 2015
      with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG target 3.3). SDG3 can
      therefore be achieved only if the NTD goals are met but, because
      interventions to tackle NTDs are widely cross-sectoral, increasing their
      global prioritization can in fact catalyse progress to achieve all SDGs
      (WHO no date b). As a result, neglected tropical diseases trap the poor in
      a cycle of poverty and disease (WHO, no date).

      At present, the World Health Organization (WHO) categorizes the following
      group of conditions as NTDs which are caused by a variety of pathogens,
      (including Viruses, Bacteria, parasites, fungi and toxins) (WHO, no date
      a)as including:

      - Buruli ulcer (Mycobacterium ulcerans infection)

      - Chagas disease (also known as American trypanosomiasis)

      - Chromoblastomycosis

      - Chikungunya (BI0203)

      - Dengue and severe dengue (BI0207)

      - Dracunculiasis (Guinea-worm disease)

      - Echinococcosis

      - Foodborne trematode infections

      - Trypanosomiasis, human African (sleeping sickness) (BI0238)

      - Leishmaniasis

      - Leprosy (BI0216)

      - Lymphatic filariasis

      - Mycetoma

      - Noma

      - Onchocerciasis

      - Podoconiosis (non-filarial lymphoedema)

      - Rabies (BI0232)

      - Scabies

      - Schistosomiasis

      - Snakebite envenoming (BI0605)

      - Soil-transmitted helminth infections

      - Sporotrichosis

      - Taeniasis/cysticercosis

      - Trachoma

      - Tungiasis

      - Yaws
