    term: Fluoride and Iodine/Iodide Excess or Inadequate Intake
    definition: >-
      Fluoride is a naturally occurring mineral to which the public are often
      exposed via drinking water. Depending on dose intake, fluoride may have
      both beneficial effects (reducing the incidence of dental caries) or
      negative effects (causing tooth enamel and skeletal fluorosis following
      prolonged high exposure) (adapted from NCBI, 2020 and WHO, no date). Some
      water supplies are fluoridated in order to achieve improved dental health.

      Iodine is a non-metallic element essential for the human body, as it is a
      crucial component of thyroid hormones, which regulate various metabolic
      processes, including growth and energy expenditure. Iodine, usually as
      iodide salts in the diet, is absorbed throughout the gastrointestinal
      tract. Iodine is essential for healthy brain development in the foetus and
      young child. A deficiency in iodine can lead to thyroid gland problems,
      such as goitre and hypothyroidism. Iodine deficiency negatively affects
      the health of women, as well as economic productivity and quality of life
      (WHO, 2023). Food fortification (often with potassium iodide, KI) is
      sometimes used to address iodine deficiency.
