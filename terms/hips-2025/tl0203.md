    term: Structural Failure
    definition: >-
      Structural failure corresponds to the exceedance of ultimate limit state
      in many of the load-carrying elements, which compromise the structural
      stability of the building (Rossetto, 2013).
    source:
      text: 'UNDRR & ISC, 2025'
      url: 'https://www.preventionweb.net/hips'
    aliases:
      - Structural collapse
      - Structural defects
      - Progressive collapse
    description: >-
      Structural failure affects both above ground and underground structures,
      including bridges, canals, viaducts, buildings, tunnels and pipelines.
      This exceedance may lead to more widespread progressive collapse of the
      wider structure.. Progressive structural collapse is defined by the
      National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) as the spread of an
      initial local failure in a manner analogous to a chain reaction that leads
      to partial or total collapse of a building (Ellingwood et al., 2007).
      Different types of progressive collapse have been referred to as pancake,
      zipper, domino, section, instability and mixed (Starossek, 2007).
