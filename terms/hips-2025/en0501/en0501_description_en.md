Biodiversity loss refers to the reduction in the variety and abundance of life on Earth, encompassing genetic, species and ecosystem levels. This decline reflects a reduction in nature's functional capacity to deliver ecosystem services, such as pollination, climate regulation, water purification and nutrient cycling. It highlights the ecological interdependence of species and the need to maintain biodiversity for human wellbeing.
Currently, more species face global extinction than ever before. Approximately 25% of assessed species groups are threatened, with up to 1 million species at risk of extinction within decades unless pressures are reduced. Extinction rates are tens to hundreds of times higher than natural background rates (IPBES, 2019).
