  en:
    term: Runoff / Nonpoint Source Pollution
    definition: >-
      Nonpoint sources of pollution refer to pollution that does not have a
      single point of origin or has not been introduced into a receiving
      freshwater or maritime environment from a specific outlet. The pollutants
      are generally carried off from the land by agricultural runoff, urban
      stormwater, atmospheric deposition or subaqueous groundwater discharges.
      The most common categories of nonpoint pollution are agriculture,
      forestry, urban areas, mining, construction, dams and channels, land
      disposal and saltwater intrusion.
