    term: Biological Agents
    definition: >-
      Biological and toxin agents are either microorganisms like viruses,
      bacteria or fungi, or toxic substances produced by living organisms that
      are produced and released deliberately to cause disease and death in
      humans, animals or plants (WHO, no date).

      Biological agents include bacteria, viruses, fungi, other microorganisms
      and their associated toxins. They have the ability to adversely affect
      human health in a variety of ways, ranging from relatively mild, allergic
      reactions to serious medical conditions-even death. In some forms,
      biological agents can also be weaponized for use in bioterrorism or other
      crimes (adapted from US OSHA, no date)
