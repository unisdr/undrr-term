    term: Non-International Armed Conflict (NIAC)
    definition: >-
      It is widely accepted today that two key conditions must be met for a
      situation of violence to be considered a Non-International Armed Conflict
      (NIAC) and therefore subject to International humanitarian law (IHL): the
      non-state party/parties must be organized, and the violence between the
      parties must be sufficiently intense. The existence of a NIAC is not
      predicated on any other threshold or condition. In particular, political
      or other motivations of the parties play no role in the classification of
      a conflict. (adapted from ICRC, 2024)
