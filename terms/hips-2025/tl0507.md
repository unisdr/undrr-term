    term: Electronic Waste (E-Waste)
    definition: >-
      Electrical and electronic waste, or E-waste, refers to electrical or
      electronic equipment that is waste, including all components,
      sub-assemblies and consumables that are part of the equipment at the time
      the equipment becomes waste (UNEP, 2019a).
    source:
      text: 'UNDRR & ISC, 2025'
      url: 'https://www.preventionweb.net/hips'
    aliases:
      - Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE)
      - E-scrap
    description: >-
      Electrical and electronic waste is classified both as hazardous waste and
      non-hazardous waste, according to the Basel Convention on the Control of
      Transboundary Movements of Hazardous Wastes and their Disposal (UNEP, no
      date a). It is categorised as hazardous waste when it belongs to any
      category in Annex I of the Basel Convention unless it does not possess any
      of the hazardous characteristics included in Annex III to the Convention.
      For example, e-waste is classified as hazardous waste when it contains
      toxic substances such as mercury, lead and brominated flame retardants
      (Basel Convention, 2020).

      Besides containing recyclable and recoverable metals and materials such as
      gold, copper, nickel, silver, rare earths and materials of strategic
      importance such as indium and palladium, e-waste can contain up to 60
      different elements from the period table, including hazardous chemicals,
      of which some are persistent organic pollutants (POPs) and dioxins listed
      under the Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants (PACE,
      2019).

      The distinction between waste and non-waste equipment is important. Some
      countries may consider used equipment destined for failure analysis,
      repair, or refurbishment as waste, while others may not. This distinction
      can affect how the material is classified and regulated.
