    term: Eutrophication
    definition: >-
      Eutrophication refers to the phenomenon of increased production of organic
      matter, primarily nitrogen and phosphorus, in aquatic systems (Nixon,
      1995). Eutrophication can be caused by human activities (e.g. sewage
      outfall, agricultural runoff, aquaculture) and may result in secondary
      environmental effects such as algal blooms and fish kills (NOAA, 2007;
      UNEP, 2015). Given the complex structure and functioning of ecosystems,
      and the multitude of pressures they face (Cloern, 2001), the precise
      definition of eutrophication remains to be established (Pannard et al.,
      2024).
