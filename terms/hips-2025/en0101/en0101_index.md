  en:
    term: Household Air Pollution
    definition: >-
      Household air pollution is pollution primarily resulting from the
      incomplete combustion of solid fuels (e.g. wood, dung, charcoal, coal,
      kerosene), resulting in the emission of potentially toxic pollutants,
      including particles of varying sizes, carbon monoxide (CO), nitrogen
      dioxide, volatile and semi-volatile organic compounds (e.g. formaldehyde
      and benzo[a]pyrene), methylene chloride and dioxins. It is one of the
      leading environmental risk factors for disease and premature death and is
      generated by the use of inefficient and polluting fuels and technologies
      in and around homes.
