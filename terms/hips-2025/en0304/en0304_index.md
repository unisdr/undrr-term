    term: Wetland Loss/Degradation
    definition: >-
      Wetland loss/degradation is a negative trend in wetland condition, caused
      by physical or direct/indirect human-induced processes, expressed as a
      long-term reduction or loss of at least one of the following: biological
      productivity, ecological role or value to humans (Olsson et al., 2019).
      Wetlands are defined as areas of marsh, fen, peatland or water, whether
      natural or artificial, permanent or temporary, with water that is static
      or flowing, fresh, brackish or salt, including areas of marine water the
      depth of which at low tide does not exceed six metres (Convention on
      Wetlands, 1971: Article 1.1). Wetlands may incorporate riparian and
      coastal zones adjacent to the wetlands, and islands or bodies of marine
      water deeper than six metres at low tide lying within the wetlands
      (Convention on Wetlands, 1971: Article 2.1).
