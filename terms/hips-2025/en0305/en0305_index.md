    term: Permafrost Loss
    definition: >-
      Permafrost is defined as ground that remains frozen at or below 0°C for a
      minimum of two consecutive years. Permafrost loss, also known as
      permafrost thaw, is the progressive loss of ground ice in permafrost,
      usually due to input of heat. Thaw can occur over decades to centuries
      over the entire depth of permafrost ground, with impacts occurring while
      thaw progresses. During thaw, temperature fluctuations are subdued because
      energy is transferred by phase change between ice and water. After the
      transition from permafrost to non-permafrost, ground can be described as
      thawed (IPCC, 2019).
