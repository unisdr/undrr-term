  en:
    term: Nuclear Agents
    definition: >-
      Nuclear agents are derived from neutron radiation (n) which is a neutron
      emitted by an unstable nucleus, in particular during atomic fission and
      nuclear fusion. Apart from a component in cosmic rays, neutrons are
      usually produced artificially. Because they are electrically neutral
      particles, neutrons can be very penetrating and when they interact with
      matter or tissue, they cause the emission of beta- and gamma-radiation.
      Neutron radiation therefore requires heavy shielding to reduce exposure
      (IAEA, 2004).
